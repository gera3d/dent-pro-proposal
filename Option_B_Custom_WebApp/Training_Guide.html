üìñ PDR Terminology
  • PDR (Paintless Dent Repair)
    A technique to remove dents from vehicle body panels without affecting the factory paint finish. Uses specialized tools to massage the metal back to its original shape.
  • DC (Dent Count)
    The total number of individual dents on a specific panel. Each dent is counted separately regardless of size. Used for pricing calculations.
  • OS (Oversized)
    Dents that exceed the standard size threshold (typically larger than a quarter or 25mm). These require additional labor and are priced higher than standard dents.
  • R&I (Remove & Install)
    Temporarily removing a part to access a dent, then reinstalling the same part. The original part is reused. Example: Removing a door panel to access the inner door skin.
  • R&R (Remove & Replace)
    Permanently removing a damaged part and replacing it with a new one. The original part is discarded. Example: Replacing a cracked mirror housing.
  • RO# (Repair Order Number)
    A unique identifier assigned to each job/vehicle. Used for tracking, billing, and communication with insurance companies.
  • Scope / Scoping
    The process of inspecting and documenting all damage on a vehicle. A thorough scope ensures accurate estimates and prevents supplements.
  • Supplement
    Additional damage discovered after the initial scope. Requires re-inspection and adjustment to the original estimate.
  • Panel
    A distinct body section of the vehicle (fender, door, hood, roof, quarter panel, etc.). Each panel is assessed and priced separately.
  • Molding (Mldg)
    Decorative or protective trim pieces attached to body panels. May need removal for dent access. Types include belt molding, side molding, and upper molding.
  • Applique
    A decorative panel or trim piece, often around windows or pillars. Usually plastic or chrome finish.
  • Liner
    Inner fender liner or wheel well liner. Plastic protective covering that may need removal for fender/quarter panel access.
  • Flare
    Fender flare or wheel arch extension. Wider trim pieces that extend the wheel opening.

  • Partial
    Initial scope in progress. Some areas still need inspection or documentation.
  • Supplement
    Additional damage found after initial scope. This form documents newly discovered issues.
üîç Damage Assessment

Each vehicle panel has two key measurements:

DC (Dent Count)

Count every individual dent on the panel. Use the +/- buttons to increment or decrement the count. Be thorough - missing dents leads to supplements.

üí° Counting Tips

Use a PDR light board or reflection to reveal all dents. Move around the panel viewing from different angles. Small creases and dings all count!

OS (Oversized)

Count dents larger than the standard threshold (usually quarter-sized or ~25mm). These are priced at a premium rate.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Oversized dents are counted IN ADDITION to the regular dent count. If you have 10 dents and 2 are oversized, enter DC: 10 and OS: 2.

Panel "Done" Checkbox

Each panel has a "Done" toggle in the header. Check this when you've completed inspecting that panel. Helps track progress on large jobs.

üîß Parts Checklist (R&I / R&R)

Each panel includes a checklist of parts that may need attention:

R&I Column (Left)

Check parts that need to be temporarily removed to access dents. These parts will be reinstalled after repair.

R&R Column (Right)

Check parts that are damaged and need replacement. New parts will be ordered and installed.

Common Parts

  • Mirror
    Side mirror assembly. R&I for door skin access, R&R if damaged.
  • Upper Mldg
    Upper door/window molding trim.
  • Belt Mldg
    Molding along the window belt line (where window meets door).
  • Trim Panel
    Interior door panel. Must be removed for inner door access.
  • Handle
    Exterior door handle.
  • Lamp(s)
    Headlights, taillights, or marker lights.
  • Emblem
    Manufacturer badges or model emblems.
  • Liner/Flare
    Wheel well liner or fender flare.

üí∞ Pricing & Real-Time Estimates

The app calculates a real-time estimate as you enter damage information. This uses industry-standard tiered pricing that reflects how most PDR shops and insurance companies calculate repairs.

Industry Standard Tiered Pricing

PDR pricing typically uses a diminishing rate structure - the more dents, the lower the per-dent rate. This reflects the efficiency gained when working on heavily damaged vehicles:

  • Tier 1: Dents 1-15 @ $75/dent
    First 15 dents are charged at the premium retail rate. This covers small door dings, parking lot damage, and light hail.
  • Tier 2: Dents 16-30 @ $60/dent
    Volume discount kicks in. Common for moderate hail damage or multiple-panel incidents.
  • Tier 3: Dents 31+ @ $45/dent
    Heavy hail/storm damage pricing. Insurance matrix rates typically fall in this range for high-count repairs.

Oversized Dent Premium

Dents larger than a quarter (approximately 1 inch / 25mm) are considered oversized and charged at 1.5√ó the tier rate (50% premium). These require more time, skill, and often multiple push points to repair.

R&I and R&R Labor

  • R&I Labor: $40/part
    Based on typical body shop labor rates ($50-65/hr) and average R&I time (0.3-1.0 hours). Covers removal for access and reinstallation.
  • R&R Labor: $55/part
    Higher rate due to additional work (unpacking new part, transfer of hardware, test fit). Does NOT include part cost.

‚ö†Ô∏è Estimate Disclaimer

The real-time estimate is a guideline based on industry averages. Actual pricing varies by: insurance company matrix rates, geographic market, vehicle type (aluminum/luxury premiums), panel accessibility, and negotiated shop rates. Always verify with your specific pricing matrix.

What's NOT Included

  • R&R Parts Cost - Labor only, not the actual cost of replacement parts
  • Aluminum/Specialty Premiums - Some vehicles require 25-50% upcharge
  • Paint/Body Work - Conventional repair costs are separate
  • Blend/Clear Coat - If paint is damaged
  • Sublet Fees - Glass, ADAS calibration, etc.

Understanding the Matrix

  • Coin Sizes

  • MCE (Most Cost Effective)
    When you see MCE on the chart, PDR should still be economical but consult personnel regarding repair viability and costs.

Sizing Dents

ü™ô Coin/Magnet Rule

When sizing dents, the coin or magnetic disk must completely cover the outer circumference or "halo" of the dent. If the quarter magnet doesn't fully cover the dent, it's a Half Dollar size.

Key Addition Rules

  • Extended Panel Repairs +25%
    Extended roofs on trucks, SUVs, pick up bedsides, panel vans, long side panels.
  • Aluminum & HSS Panels +25%
    High Strength Steel (HSS) and Aluminum panels require more skill and time.
  • Double Panels +25%
    Roof rails, upper door frames - panels that are doubled/layered.
  • Maximum 35% Upcharge
    When 2+ add-ons apply to a panel, max is 35% (25% first, 10% second).
  • Oversized Dent (OS) +$50/dent
    Dent exceeds Half Dollar size but is smaller than two half dollars combined.
  • Double Oversized (DOS) +$100/dent
    Dent exceeds 2 half dollars in size.
  • Corrosion Protection +$10/vehicle
    When applicable for exposed metal areas.

‚úÖ Recommended Workflow
  1. Start with vehicle info - Enter RO#, VIN, Year/Make/Model first. This ensures the form is identifiable if you need to save and return later.
  2. Take VIN/Odometer photos - Use the camera button to capture verification photos before starting the damage inspection.
  3. Work systematically - Start at one corner of the vehicle and work around methodically. Don't jump between panels.
  4. Count dents carefully - Use proper lighting. Count all dents first (DC), then identify oversized ones (OS).
  5. Mark "Done" on each panel - Check the Done box as you complete each panel to track progress.
  6. Check R&I/R&R needs - For each panel with dents, consider what parts need removal for access or replacement if damaged.
  7. Take damage photos - Photograph each damaged area. More photos = fewer disputes later.
  8. Review stats before submitting - Check the bottom bar totals. Do they match what you observed?
  9. Save locally, then submit - Save to local storage first as a backup, then submit to the system.
‚≠ê Pro Tips
üî¶ Lighting is Everything

Use a PDR light board or work in conditions with good reflection. Dents are nearly invisible in flat lighting. The best scopes happen with proper lighting setup.

üì± Save Frequently

Hit Save after completing each major section. If you get interrupted or the app crashes, you won't lose your work.

üì∏ Photo Documentation

Take more photos than you think you need. Include: VIN plate, odometer, overall vehicle shots, and close-ups of each damaged area. Photos protect you during disputes.

üîç Check Body Lines

Dents along body lines, character lines, and edges are often harder to repair. Note these in the Comments section as they may affect pricing.

üìù Use Notes Wisely

The Notes field is for anything that doesn't fit elsewhere: prior damage, access concerns, customer requests, or unusual conditions.

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Undercount

It's better to slightly overcount than undercount. Undercounting leads to supplements, delays, and unhappy customers. When in doubt, count it!
